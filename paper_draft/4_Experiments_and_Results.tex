\section{Experiments and Results}
\label{sec:experiments_results}

\subsection{Experimental Protocol}

We evaluate on a unified four-class emotion space (\textit{angry / happy / neutral / sad}) using weighted accuracy (WA) and unweighted average recall (UAR).
The backbone is frozen WavLM Base+ with a learnable 12-layer weighted sum, followed by one of two parameter-matched pooling heads (\textbf{111,105 parameters each}), and an SEMLP classifier ($\sim$590K parameters).

Two regularization profiles are used.
The \textit{default} profile (weight decay $10^{-3}$, label smoothing 0.1, no pooling dropout) is applied to acted corpora (C-BESD, IEMOCAP) to preserve baseline comparability.
The \textit{fau} profile (weight decay $5\times10^{-3}$, label smoothing 0.15, pooling dropout 0.3, gradient clipping 1.0) is applied to spontaneous FAU Aibo experiments, targeting the rapid overfitting observed in preliminary runs.

All splits are speaker-disjoint (70/15/15, deterministic MD5 hash).

\paragraph{Acted child speech (C-BESD).}
On this acted corpus, pure self-attention pooling reaches \textbf{92.78\%} WA, marginally outperforming prosody-guided pooling by +1.48 percentage points.
Both systems achieve near-identical UAR ($\sim$92.8\% vs.\ 91.4\%), confirming that the acted recording protocol produces highly prototypical acoustic patterns that a sufficiently expressive attention head can memorize without explicit prosodic guidance.
The self-attention model requires 30 epochs to converge, whereas prosody-guided pooling peaks at epoch 10---three times faster---suggesting that prosodic features provide a stronger inductive bias that accelerates convergence.

\paragraph{Adult speech (IEMOCAP).}
Prosody-guided pooling on adult spontaneous speech (Exp3) yields 58.67\% WA / 59.11\% UAR, a 32.63pp drop from the acted child speech setting.
This confirms that the prosody pathway captures patterns specific to \textit{children's} vocal characteristics (wider F0 range, higher variability), which do not transfer to adult speech.

\paragraph{Zero-shot cross-domain (C-BESD $\rightarrow$ FAU Aibo).}
The most striking result is Exp4: a model trained on acted child speech and evaluated directly on spontaneous child speech achieves only \textbf{19.56\%} WA---below the 25\% chance baseline for four classes, on held-out test speakers (N=3389).
This catastrophic failure demonstrates that acted-speech priors are fundamentally incompatible with naturalistic child vocalizations across language boundaries.

\paragraph{In-domain spontaneous (FAU Aibo).}
Under the stronger \textit{fau} regularization profile, prosody-guided pooling narrowly leads in WA (\textbf{66.36\%} vs.\ 66.18\%, $\Delta$=+0.18pp), while self-attention achieves higher UAR (58.23\% vs.\ 56.35\%).
Both models peak within 2--3 epochs despite 100 available, confirming that rapid overfitting on naturalistic child speech remains the primary bottleneck, independent of pooling architecture.

\subsection{Convergence and Regularization Effects}

The \textit{fau} regularization profile successfully extended the useful training window from a single-epoch spike (observed in preliminary unregularized runs) to 2--3 epochs, but did not fundamentally resolve the data-hunger problem inherent in small spontaneous child speech corpora.
Notably, the label smoothing (0.15) and gradient clipping (1.0) components prevented training divergence that occurred in 4 out of 10 preliminary unregularized FAU runs.

\subsection{Cross-Dataset Summary}

Three distribution-shift dimensions emerge from the matrix:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Speech style} (acted $\rightarrow$ spontaneous): 92.78\% $\rightarrow$ 19.56\% zero-shot; 66.36\% in-domain.
\item \textbf{Age group} (child $\rightarrow$ adult): 91.30\% $\rightarrow$ 58.67\% (prosody path).
\item \textbf{Corpus scale}: C-BESD (2,780 samples) supports 30+ training epochs; FAU Aibo (18,216 samples) paradoxically peaks in 2--3 epochs, likely due to high intra-class acoustic variability in spontaneous recordings.
\end{enumerate}
